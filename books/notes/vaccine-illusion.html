1. How We Got Married to the Idea of Vaccination

Originates from variolation. Edward Jenner standardized vaccinations after he injected subjects with a cowpox pustule and prematurely declared, as a result of his vaccination, and convinced British authorities to adopt vaccinations. Mr. Jenner did not test his vaccination against the naturally occuring smallpox but pus from a smallpox pustule. Through his inactions, he was not able to discover that the protection from the vaccine merely postpones a person's susceptibility to smallpox.

Emil von Behring and Shibasaburo Kitasato utilized horse anti-serum for treating diphtheria and tetanus.
